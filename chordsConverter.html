CHORDS CONVERTER

Product Design & Website development

Chords Converter is a digital tool imagined for musicians who wish to learn and practice music, together or in a band :)

Try it!

Project Origin

This project was born from a personal need as a guitarist. I never use a capo to transpose chords; instead, I manually transpose them in my head while learning songs. This method works for simple songs, but becomes challenging with complex melodies. Avoiding the capo, I prefer barre chords to fully explore the guitar‚Äôs tonal range. Most online tools either don‚Äôt offer chord transposition or require a subscription. That‚Äôs why I created this free chord converter.

Why?

I refuse to use a capo because I consider it like "cutting off" the guitar neck and suppressing notes. I always prefer to use barre chords to enjoy all the possibilities and tones that the guitar can offer.

Most of the websites I use for tablatures don‚Äôt offer a solution to transpose chords, or it‚Äôs a special feature that you need to subscribe to. That‚Äôs why I decided to build this chord converter.

First version:

Initially, I developed this tool using Angular. The chord selection tool was managed using the Select2 plugin. However, the chords were separated from the song's lyrics, led me to rethink the design. I decided to remake it using React, as I think it offers more flexibility, especially since I developed this app on the fly, without any previous sketches or wireframes (I hadn't studied UI/UX design yet üòä).

Changes made

  • The heart of the app ‚Äì the Chord Converter: This section allows users to copy-paste a tablature (the chords and lyrics) and specify where the capo should be placed to get the correct tuning. Once these details are entered, the user can start learning the song right away.

I added a parallax effect to make the design feel more modern and to offer an immersive scrolling experience, making the interaction with the site more dynamic.
